Project Overview

The project aims to mitigate human-wildlife conflicts by providing early warning, efficient wildlife surveillance, and promoting sustainable coexistence between local communities and wildlife.

The system is designed to protect both villagers and wildlife by providing early warnings and reducing the chances of dangerous encounters. This initiative is part of a broader effort to promote coexistence and conservation in the region.

Our Technology

We use advanced thermal imaging cameras and species identification algorithms to monitor wildlife activity. Geofencing technology creates virtual boundaries around village borders, triggering alerts when breached by high-priority animals.

Our technology stack includes:

  • High-resolution thermal imaging cameras
  • Machine learning algorithms for species identification
  • Geofencing software for virtual boundaries
  • Real-time data processing and SMS alert systems
